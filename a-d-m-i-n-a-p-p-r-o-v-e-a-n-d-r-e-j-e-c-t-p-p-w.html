SCHOOL OF COMPUTING DEANS AWARD
The Dean’s Award for Excellence aims to recognize and reward outstanding achievements by students, faculty, or staff in the fields of academia, research, community service, or leadership within the institution. This prestigious accolade highlights exemplary contributions that align with the university’s mission of fostering innovation, promoting academic excellence, and encouraging civic engagement. Recipients of the Dean’s Award will be honored at the annual university awards ceremony, where their accomplishments will be celebrated by the academic community.

The primary objective of the Dean’s Award is to acknowledge and commend individuals who have demonstrated exceptional performance and dedication in their respective areas. This award seeks to:

  1. Inspire continued excellence and motivation among the university community.
  2. Highlight the importance of contributions that significantly impact the institution and society.
  3. Encourage a culture of recognition and appreciation within the university.
Established in 1998, the Dean’s Award has become one of the most esteemed honors within the university. Over the years, this award has recognized a diverse group of individuals, from pioneering researchers to dedicated community volunteers, all of whom have made significant contributions to the university and beyond. The award highlights the university’s commitment to fostering a supportive and enriching environment where excellence is nurtured and celebrated.

The aim of the Dean’s Award is to:

  1. Identify and celebrate individuals who exemplify the highest standards of excellence in their endeavors.
  2. Provide a platform for the university to publicly acknowledge and reward significant contributions made by its members.
  3. Enhance the visibility of outstanding achievements and promote a culture of aspiration and success within the university community.
